Rules
  • •  Exactly one active item, marked by the gradient pill.
  • •  Inactive items use Ink-soft text + outline icons; hover tints them pale blue.
  • •  A hairline divider separates primary nav from elevated roles (HOD / Admin).
  • •  Log out is pinned to the bottom as the black utility anchor.

Who We Build For

Five roles, one platform. Every screen should answer one of these people’s core emotional need at a glance.

1
ProfessorPrimary operational user
Pain points
  • Missing schedule updates
  • Last-minute extra classes
  • Unclear invigilation duties
  • Too many channels, no single truth
Goals
  • Know today’s responsibilities instantly
  • Never miss a class or duty
  • View timetable easily
  • Respond to requests quickly
Emotional need“Just tell me what I need to do today.”
2
Academic CoordinatorPower user
Pain points
  • Endless follow-ups
  • Faculty not responding
  • Timetable conflicts
  • Manual spreadsheet juggling
Goals
  • Keep operations running smoothly
  • Quickly fill schedule gaps
  • Track pending requests
  • Reduce manual coordination
Emotional need“Help me stop chasing people.”
3
HODDepartment leadership
Pain points
  • Limited visibility
  • Learns about issues too late
  • Can’t monitor workload split
  • Difficult approval tracking
Goals
  • Understand department status instantly
  • Identify bottlenecks
  • Ensure class coverage
  • Manage faculty workload fairly
Emotional need“I want visibility without micromanaging.”
4
DeanCross-department leadership
Pain points
  • Fragmented reporting
  • Department silos
  • Delayed information
Goals
  • Cross-department visibility
  • Faster decision making
  • Compliance monitoring
Emotional need“Show me where intervention is needed.”
5
VC / Institute LeadershipRuns the institution
Pain points
  • Lack of operational visibility
  • Reliance on second-hand reports
  • Hidden inefficiencies
Goals
  • Monitor institute health
  • Identify systemic bottlenecks
  • Track execution
Emotional need“Give me confidence the institution is functioning.”
